Accessibility

Accessibility in website operation is an important target for all developers. Accessibility is determining “how a disabled person accesses or benefits from a site.” Disabilities including those of a visual, auditory, or cognitive nature can impair one’s ability to use a site unless that site has been engineered with these cases in mind. In some aspects, accessible sites are easier for non-impaired users such as those on mobile devices, which typically have smaller screens that make content harder to see. Other examples are easier content comprehension when content is made simpler to understand, and when sites have a clear and logical operation. Alt text and text on the page help to make sites as easy as possible to use. Additionally sites that have audio or visual content should be careful about the effect the content could have on people with epilepsy or who are prone to seizures.

Since one of the core features of the internet is its creation of an interconnected community that transcends physicality, accessibility enables more people to contribute to and benefit from that community. Important services the internet offers are thus available to more people, including some services that are specifically for people with the disabilities that accessibility is geared towards (e.g. those with poor vision and online optometry resources). Since accessibility makes sites available to more people, it is a good business choice to make a site as accessible to everyone as possible.

Different methods for making sites more accessible include the use of sounds, large, easy-to-read fonts, and avoiding using colors as main navigational tools. Besides this, much of accessible site content is just straightforward, well-implemented and designed code. The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) has established best practices for a multitude of web development and design aspects, and many of these are suitable for creating accessible sites. Implementing these practices and designing a site from the onset with accessibility in mind make it easier than ever to provide practicable websites.
